# The Trouble With Being Born

Another title for the book would have been *The Trouble With Being*. I read this book after reading it alongside "*The Unbearable Lightness of Being*" because I thought it would be funny to have read two books with clearly opposite perspectives of "*being*".

I say this book could be "*The Trouble With Being*" because Cioran doesn't have a problem with birth, but existence. The question of "*Being*", of existing as a human subject, and other questions of the same sense have been a constant throughout the human canon. Buddhism's "*illusory self*", "*existentialism*" or "*the absurd*", "*metaphysics*" in some ways, *Heidegger*'s entire project, and even famous literature.

> "To be or not to be, that is the question."
> <figcaption class="blockquote-footer">Shakespeare</figcaption>

People, regardless of race, culture, or whatever ideological boundary, come face-to-face with what they will do in life or what they have been put here to do. Religion provides some insight, time does as well, but in the end it doesn't seem to be *an Answer* that everyone can agree with.[^1]
