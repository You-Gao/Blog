# A Random Walk Down Wall Street

> "Never buy anything from someone who is out of breath."
> <figcaption class="blockquote-footer">A Random Walk Down Wall Street</figcaption>

The book introduces concepts of investing and theories of value in a digestible way. It discusses practical tips for where to park your money alongside the economic rationale to do so. The parts I'd like to understand more of and therefore write on is (1): theories of value and (2): fundamental vs. technical trading.

Starting with theories of value. "*Random Walk Down Wall Street*", introduces two differing theories of asset valuation at the start of the book; "*Firm-Foundation*" and "*Castle-in-the-Sky*". The conceptual distinction decided by if markets operates on myth or reality. What I see is here though, is at the base of it, the two theories have to answer to the question of value assignment.
