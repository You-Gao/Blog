# learning from job applications

Another New Year's reflection. Last New Year's I was thinking about the potential coursework and things I [could be or wanted to be learning](https://blog.yougao.dev/life/future-studies/).

This year I have started filling gaps in knowledge. A good way to do that was looking through job applications in the tech industry. CS curriculums are designed from both a bottom-up and top-down approach. However, there are some concepts at the bottom and some software at the top remain untaught. 

As a CS student, you don't study the "*basic*" installation of physical infrastructure nor do you study the commonly deployed software systems. A CS degree doesn't prep you for an "*IT*", "*sysadmin*", or "*DevOps*" role which makes sense as most come out as software developers.
